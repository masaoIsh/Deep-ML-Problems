In the comparison shown in Figure 2.2, which method will perform best in the long run in terms of cumulative reward and probability of selecting the best action? How much better will it be? Express your answer quantitatively.

A: In a setup with k=4 arms, the epsilon=0.1 will choose the best arm with a probability of 0.9+0.1*0.25=0.9+0.025=0.925=92.5%. The epsilon=0.01 will choose the best arm with a probability of 0.99+0.01*0.25=0.99+0.0025=0.9925=99.25%. Thus, in the long run, epsilon=0.01 will perform best.
